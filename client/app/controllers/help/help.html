Background

During emergencies, a wealth of information relevant to humanitarian decision makers is available through news media, NGOs reports, social media, meeting minutes, government press release, etc. Unfortunately, this information is not systematically captured and analysed in real time, mainly due to a lack of time. Capturing this information requires manpower and a collaborative, systematic and thorough way of screening all new available information, tagging them by field of interest and storing them in a tabular format to ease further analysis.

The Humanitarian Information Review and Analysis platform was developed to ease the process of data capture and tagging during emergencies and ensuring no important information is missed. It was developed by and for the Digital Humanitarian Network and will hopefully fill an important technological gap in the way humanitarian operators capture and perform secondary data review.

The platform is administered by a group of three organizations, namely DHN, OCHA and ACAPS who are responsible for the appropriate and safe use, maintenance and development of the platform.

Platform code contributed by Itamar Maltz, Source code is available at https://github.com/ism-qdatum/hira/

Guidence

This section provides guidance to users on how to use the different menu and options available in HIRA to capture information in a similar way across users. In order to use the platform, you need:

  • One document or one web page opened with the information of interest
  • The HIRA platform opened (http://dnu.lab.oii.me) where you are logged in. You can register automatically by clicking the Dont have a user? Click here to register in the main page.
Source Page

The sources page provide a detailed list of all source documents currently in the system, with some key metadata (Source, date, URL, etc.).

The first step in HIRA is to enter the source. The source can be a physical document (PDF, Word doc) in your computer or an extract/excerpt from a webpage or a twitter, etc. Proceed as follow to add a document into HIRA:

  1. Click on the button
  2. A blank row appear in the summary table for your new document, with the fields date, type, site, URL, Document and countries to fill:
  3. Enter the date and the information source name, using the menus
  4. In case the document is available from internet, enter the URL (copy/paste from your browser). The site main URL will automatically be extracted and entered into the “Site” column.
  5. Click the blue button “Save”
  6. In case the source is an actual document (PDF, word, excel, etc.), upload the document directly into HIRA, clicking on the button
  7. At this stage, the source document is uploaded into the HIRA platform. Now, one document will generally contain multiple observations, paragraphs or sentences of interest for you. Each of those excerpts needs to be tagged according to some pre-defined topics of interest. To go to the HIRA interface allowing for tagging the excerpts, click on the 0Add Entry button. Observe that at this stage, no excerpt has been attached to this source, so the number “0” appear at the left of the “Add entry” text.
Tagging Page

In this window, we have a simple interface where a selected piece of text can receive several tags. Proceed as follow to tag appropriately the information:

  1. Select/copy from your document or your webpage the piece of information of interest, that will be call the “excerpt”. Paste the piece of information into the Excerpt window, i.e.:
  2. The first step is to enter the location to which the information refer to. You can select the country and an admin level 2 or 3 in the dropdown menu. Location is a mandatory field, at least the country name should be entered. It is possible to enter multiple locations for a same excerpt, i.e. if two districts are referenced in the excerpt or if two countries are mentioned. You can delete a location by clicking on the red button at the right of the menus.
  3. You need now to tag the information according to several fields/attributes of interest: the sector(s), the type of information, the response category it refers to, if the excerpt have reference to vulnerable or affected groups, etc. A pre-defined list for each of those categories is available in the tab “Definitions”. You have the option to add or remove categories if necessary. However, it is strongly advised to consult with the administrators before to do so as some of those categories are mutually exclusive and standards in the humanitarian sector.
  4. Additionally, for each piece of information, the reliability level of the information and the severity of conditions needs to be judged.
  5. For the purpose of the HIRA, the reliability of information is determined based on the following scale (Adapted from the Canadian Source reliability and Information accuracy rating matrix 2013):
    • 1. Completely reliable. No doubt of authenticity, trustworthiness, or competency; has a history of complete reliability
    • 2. Usually reliable. Minor doubt about authenticity, trustworthiness, or competency Has a history of valid information most of the time
    • 3. Fairly reliable. Doubt of authenticity, trustworthiness, or competency but has provided valid information in the past
    • 4. Not usually reliable. Significant doubt about authenticity, trustworthiness, or competency but has provided valid information in the past
    • 5. Unreliable. Lacking in authenticity, trustworthiness, and competency; history of invalid information
    • 6.Cannot be judged. No basis exists for evaluating the reliability of the information
  6. Under HIRA, the severity of conditions is based on the following severity scale, designed to categorise the severity of the problems mentioned and the urgency of intervention required. Severity is defined here as the degree to which an immediate intervention is required to avoid harmful consequences.
    7. Security Level Intensity Action Needed When
    Level 1: No problem. Normal or near normal situation for 'sector name'. Population is living under normal conditions. All or nearly all 'sector name' needs are met. No action required Normal Situation No
    Level 2: Minor problem: Situation of minor concern for 'sector name'. People are facing problems or shortages in 'sector name' but they are not life threatening. Affected population is feeling the strain of the situation but can cope with the current situation with local resources. No action required. Minor disruptions No
    Level 3 Situation of concern for 'sector name'. People are facing 'sector name' problems or shortages causing discomfort and suffering, but they are not life threatening. Affected population can cope with the current situation with local resources but conditions may turn concerning and situation needs to be monitored carefully. Moderate/stressed/situation of concern ASAP
    Level 4: Situation of major concern for 'sector name'. People are facing 'Sector name' problems or shortages causing discomfort and/or high level of suffering which can result in irreversible damages to health status, but they are not life threatening. Affected population will not be able to cope with the 'Sector name' current conditions if the situation persists and no external humanitarian assistance is being provided. External assistance is required. Serious, but not life threatening Intervention Short-middle term
    Level 5: Severe conditions. As a result of shortages, the affectedpopulation faces life-threatening conditions causing high level of suffering and irreversible damages to health status, which can result in deaths if no external humanitarian assistance is provided soon. External assistance risks Severe (potentially life threatening) Intervention Short-term
    Level 6: Critical situation for 'sector name'. As a result of shortages, the affected population faces life-threatening conditions causing high level of suffering, irreversible damages to health status and deaths. Deaths are already reported, directly caused by the current 'sector name' conditions, and more deaths are expected if no immediate 'sector name' assistance is provided. External assistance is very urgently required. Critical (life threatening) Intervention ASAP
  7. One excerpt might require entering several “lines of tags”. This is particularly the case if the excerpt depict situations with various level of severity. Ultimately, there should be as many lines of tags as there is different degree of severity referred to. I.e. if one excerpt establishes that “Residents face an acute lack of water causing already several fatalities and high level of dehydration, particularly among children and pregnant women, and that access to food for the IDPs remains of concern for the authorities”. This excerpt should be tagged as follows:
    • One line of tags for WASH/Water supply, Current problem, children/Pregnant women, severity level 4.
    • When the tags have been entered, click on the green button at the bottom saying “Save & Add new tags”, in order to be able to enter a new line of tags. Since the excerpt reference a situation of lesser severity in the food sector, we need now to enter a line of tag for Food/ access, Current problem, IDPs, severity level 3:
    • At the end of the process and when no further lines of tags are necessary, click on the blue button “Save”. This should bring you back to the Sources menu. From there you can chose either to enter a new entry for the same document (i.e. if another paragraph need to be captured) or to load a new source in the HIRA and restart the tagging process.
